1. Getting started & logging in

Anyone can view everything in the app without an account — fixtures, results, player profiles, league tables. No login needed to browse.

To make changes you need to sign in. There are two account types:

RoleWhat they can do
Admin Full access — create and edit leagues, teams, players, fixtures; manage all squads and scores; manage user accounts.
Captain Restricted access — can manage squads, enter scores, and record match fees for fixtures involving their own team only. Cannot create or delete anything else.

Signing in works the same way for both roles:

  1. Click Sign in in the top-right corner of any page.
  2. Enter your email address and click Send sign-in link.
  3. Open the email from Firebase and click the link — you'll be signed in automatically.
  4. You'll be returned to wherever you were.

No password is required. The sign-in link can only be used once and expires shortly after it's sent.

After signing in, your role is shown in the top-right corner — admins see the Admin nav link, captains see a Captain badge.

To sign out, click Sign out in the top-right corner. Your session expires after 10 minutes of inactivity.

Only one user can be logged in at a time. If someone is already logged in, the sign-in page will show a message with the time remaining on their session. A new login is only possible once that session expires or the current user logs out.

Accounts must be created by an existing admin — see Managing accounts.

2. The dashboard (home page)

The home page gives a quick overview of the club's activity.

Stats row — Shows the number of leagues, teams, and fixtures recorded in total.

Last 7 Days — Lists fixtures from the past week with scores. Click any fixture row to open its detail page. If there are more than three, click "Show more" to expand.

Next 7 Days — Shows upcoming fixtures so you know what's coming up.

Click View All to go to the full fixture list.

3. Leagues

Navigate to Leagues from the top nav bar. Click a league name to open its detail page, which shows divisions, teams, and eligibility rules.

Creating a league admin only

Fill in the New League form at the bottom of the Leagues page:

FieldWhat it means
Namee.g. "Men's Winter League"
Season start dateThe Saturday that Round 1 begins — used to compute round labels on fixtures
Round 5 start dateThe Saturday Round 5 begins; the week before it is automatically used as the first catch-up week
Round 9 start dateThe Saturday Round 9 begins; the week before it is automatically used as the second catch-up week
Doubles pairs per roundHow many pairs play each match (usually 3)
Early season weeksRounds where the "one fixture per player per week" rule applies
Team tie thresholdMatches played before a player is locked to that team
Number of divisionsHow many divisions to create immediately

Other league actions admin only

  • Edit League — update any of the fields above from the league detail page.
  • Add Division — type a division name and click Add on the league detail page.
  • Assign teams — use the team checkboxes within a division and click Save Teams.
  • Delete League — removes the league and all its fixtures permanently.
4. Teams

Navigate to Teams from the top nav bar.

The Teams page has two sections: a team list at the top, and a squad allocation view below — a week-by-week breakdown of upcoming fixtures per league where you can reserve players.

Creating a team admin only

Use the New Team form: enter a name, optionally assign a division, and tick Match fees enabled if this team tracks fee payments. Click Create Team.

Team detail page

Click a team name to see its roster, fixtures, and current captain.

The player roster shows each player's Wins, Losses, and Win % in two figures: the bold number is their record for this team, and the number in brackets is their record across all leagues combined. For example, 3 (10) means 3 rubbers won for this team and 10 rubbers won in total.

Editing a team requires login

The Edit button (top-right of the team detail page) lets you rename the team and toggle match fees. Both admins and the team's own captain can do this.

Other team actions admin only

  • Add players — use the Add Players dropdown on the team detail page.
  • Remove a player — click the remove icon next to their name.
  • Set captain — click the star icon next to a player. A confirmation prompt will appear before the change is saved; clicking again (and confirming) removes them as captain. Only one captain per team is allowed — assigning a new one replaces the existing captain.
  • Delete team — removes the team and all its fixtures.
5. Players

Navigate to Players from the top nav bar. Two views are available via the toggle buttons at the top right: Players (the default roster table) and Eligibility (the grid described below).

The Players view shows every club member with their gender, membership status, teams, fixtures played, and season ties. Use the filter boxes above each column to search.

Eligibility Grid

Switch to the Eligibility view to see a cross-table of every player (interested in teams) against every team in leagues that have eligibility restrictions configured. Rows are players; columns are teams, grouped by league.

Use the Men / Women filter to show only one gender. Click any team column header to highlight that column.

Each cell shows how many fixtures that player has played for that team, colour-coded by status:

BadgeMeaning
Empty green outlineEligible — no appearances yet
Green numberEligible — N fixtures played (not yet at tie threshold)
Yellow numberTied to this team — threshold reached
Faded strikethrough numberBlocked — tied to a different team at the same or a lower division

The grid only appears when at least one league has a team tie threshold set. Players not marked as "interested in team play" are excluded.

Player detail page

Click a player's name to see their match history, win/loss record, eligibility status per league, and match fee history.

If the player has an LTA number recorded, it appears as a button below their name — click it to copy to clipboard. If the player has a contact telephone recorded, that appears alongside it, but is only visible to admins and captains who manage a team the player belongs to.

Adding a player admin only

Click Add Player on the Players page and fill in:

FieldNotes
First & last nameRequired
GenderM or F
Membership statusActive or Inactive
Interested in team playYes or No — controls whether they appear in squad dropdowns
LTA numberOptional but recommended
Contact telephoneOptional — only visible to admins and captains of the player's teams

If a similar name already exists, the app will warn you before creating a duplicate.

Editing & deleting admin only

Use the Edit or Delete buttons on a player's detail page. Deleting removes the player and all their match records; team rosters are unaffected.

6. Fixtures

Navigate to Fixtures from the top nav bar. Fixtures are grouped by calendar week (Saturday–Friday). The current week is highlighted and scrolled into view automatically.

Each row shows the date, home vs away team, score (or a stopwatch if unplayed), round label, and league. Click either team name to open the fixture from that team's perspective.

Use the League and Team filter dropdowns at the top to narrow the list.

Adding a fixture admin only

Import — upload a .ics file exported from Google Calendar. The app matches team names automatically, shows a review screen, and skips duplicates.

Add Fixture — manually enter a date, home team, away team, and league, then click Save Fixture. The correct number of rubber slots is created automatically.

Bulk operations admin only

Tick fixture checkboxes (or use Select all), then use Set League to reassign fixtures in bulk, or Delete to remove them all at once.

7. Entering match results

Open a fixture from the Fixtures list or a team page. The Pairs table shows one row per pair, with three set columns (one rubber per column).

How scoring works

Each pair plays three rubbers against different opposition pairs. For each rubber:

  • Enter your team's score (left) and the opposition score (right).
  • T — marks the rubber as a tie (no score needed).
  • W — marks a walkover. Click once for a Yarm walkover, again to switch to an opposition walkover, again to clear.

Scoring: 2 pts per rubber win · 1 pt each for a tie · 4 bonus pts to the team winning more rubbers

Steps to enter results captain or admin

  1. Select players — choose two players per row from the dropdowns. Score boxes are locked until both are selected.
  2. Enter scores — fill in (or mark T/W) every rubber. The Save button activates only when all cells are complete.
  3. Click Save Results. The overall fixture score updates automatically.

Editing fixture details captain or admin

Edit (top-right) — change the date, teams, league, or overall score. The Round field defaults to auto (derived from the league's start date); pick a specific round to override it for rearranged matches. Captains can edit fixtures involving their team.

Clearing or deleting admin only

  • Clear (below the table) — resets all scores and clears squad selections.
  • Delete (top-right) — removes the fixture and all results. Player profiles are preserved.
8. Managing squads

A squad is the list of players reserved for a fixture before it's played — useful for planning and flagging eligibility issues in advance.

Squads can be managed from the Teams page (week-by-week allocation view) or from an individual fixture detail page.

Selecting players captain or admin

In the Squad section on a fixture page, tick the players you want and click Save Squad. Each player shows a status icon:

IconMeaning
✓ GreenTied to this team for the season — all good
⚠ YellowSelecting this player will tie them to this team — they'll be blocked from lower divisions after this
⊘ RedIneligible — already tied to a different team at the same or lower division

If a tie would be triggered, a confirmation popup explains this before saving.

Eligibility rules

  • Early season weeks — during the first N rounds (set per league), a player can only appear in one fixture per week across all teams.
  • Team tie — after playing the threshold number of matches for a team, a player is locked to that team's division tier. They can still be picked for a higher division, but not a lower one.

A player's current tie status is shown on their individual player page.

9. Match fees

If a team has match fees enabled, a Match Fees section appears on the fixture detail page for captains and admins.

Tick a player's checkbox to mark their fee as paid, then click Save Fees. A running count of paid vs outstanding is shown in the section header.

Match fees can be toggled on or off per team via the team's Edit settings.

11. Managing accounts

Account management is available via Admin in the top nav bar (visible to admins only). All user management is on a single User Access page — admins and captains are registered here together.

Adding an admin admin only

  1. Go to Admin → User Access.
  2. Enter the new admin's email address, select Admin as the access level, and click Add user.
  3. Tell them to go to /login, enter their email, and click the sign-in link that Firebase emails them.

No password is set — sign-in always uses an emailed link. Any Firebase user not assigned as a captain is treated as an admin.

Adding a captain admin only

Captain accounts give a team captain access to manage their team's fixtures without full admin privileges.

  1. Make sure the captain has a Player profile in the app and is set as the captain of their team on the team detail page.
  2. Go to Admin → User Access, enter their email, select Captain as the access level, and choose their linked player from the dropdown.
  3. Click Add user. Their Firebase account and captain role are created in one step.
  4. Tell them to go to /login and sign in with their email.

On sign-in, they will have captain-level access. If a captain is linked to a player who captains multiple teams, they can manage fixtures for all of those teams.

Removing a user admin only

On the User Access page, click the button next to the account and confirm. The Firebase account is deleted immediately — they will no longer be able to sign in. You cannot delete your own account.

12. Login activity login required

The Activity page (visible in the top nav bar when signed in) shows all sign-in sessions from the last 30 days.

For each session it shows:

  • The email address of the user who signed in
  • Their role at the time of the session (Admin, Captain, or Viewer)
  • When they signed in and when they signed out
  • The total session duration
  • Whether the session ended via an explicit sign-out or timed out due to inactivity

This page is visible to all signed-in users (admins and captains) for transparency and auditing purposes.
